Lasanga Recipe


This recipe is so good, I have modified it over the years to get even better results. After working with some Michelin starred chefs to
refine this recipe I think you will really enjoy it. It does take some time but the effort is worth it!

  • Prep:30 mins
  • Cook:2 hrs 30 mins
  • Total:3 hrs
  • Serves:12

Ingredients

  • 1 lb. Italian Sausage
  • 1 lb. Ground Beef
  • 2 large yellow onions
  • 1 large carrot
  • 5 cloves of garlic
  • 64 ounces of Tomato sauce
  • 1 bunch of parsley
  • 1 bunch of basil
  • 1 ounce of oregano
  • 1 lb. of lasanga sheets
  • cumin tt
  • nutmeg tt
  • Salt tt
  • 1 cup ricotta
  • 1 lb. mozarella
  • 1 lb. spinach
  • 1/4 lb. parmesan
  • 2 ounces of white wine
  • red pepper flakes tt
  • 2 ounces olive oil

Directions


  1. First we need to bring a large pot of water to a boil and cook the lasanga sheets, I generally add 2 tablespoons
    of salt for every four quarts of water. You will need about 8 quarts of water to make this an easy cook.
    While the water is coming to a boil you can dice your onions, carrots, and herbs. If your feeling abitious you
    you can sautee or brown the meat including sausage. Once that is done remove the meat from the pan.
  2. Next you can sautee the onions and carrots in olive oil and use the same pan you cooked the meat in
    do this until lightly translucent then add the wine for a second and then you scrap the bottom to get all
    the good stuff off the bottom of the pan. Now you can add the meat mix back into the pan with the tomato
    sauce with all the herbs and garlic. Salt to taste at the very end, not before.
  3. Now once the pasta is done and the sauce is going you can grate your cheese and begin lining your
    baking dish, first I spread a little oil on the sides and bottom of the dish, then lay a sheet down.
    Next i start to layer my ingredients. I go pasta, sauce, spinach, cheese, a little bit of sauce then
    pasta again and repeat. Top layer should be pasta with a little bit sauce and cheese, this gives it a
    good crispy layer and helps to hold everything together.
  4. Let it cool for fifteen to thirty minutes so it gels together better before cutting and serving.
    This dish can be portioned and frozen to enjoy for later as well. You can also substitute dried pasta
    sheets for freshly made ones just reduce the cooking time to 2 minutes maximum. Dried sheets take anywhere
    between 8 to 10 minutes to cook.
